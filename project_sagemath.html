This project is done as a "Projct with Colloqium" for my Bachelor's degree from TUM

Algebraic Number Theory quite often deals with the Ring of Integers \(O_K\) of an imaginary-quadratic Number Field \(K=\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{-d})\). Not mentioned as often, but also important are the Orders of \(K\): Subrings \(O \subseteq O_K\) that are also rank 2 free \(\mathbb{Z}\)-modules. Analogously to the Ideal Class Group \(Cl(K)\), we can define an Ideal Class Group of an Order \(C(O)\). If \(O \subset O'\) are two Orders, we get an induced surjective Homomorphism \(C(O') \rightarrow C(O)\)

In this Project, I'm implementing an efficient way of (1) evaluating this Group Homomorphism, (2) determining the Kernel of it, (3) identifying short relations, and (4) representing the Kernel in some Finite Field
